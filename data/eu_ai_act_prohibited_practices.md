# Prohibited AI practices

Article 5 prohibits placing on the market, putting into service, or using an AI system that uses
subliminal techniques beyond a person's consciousness, or purposefully manipulative or deceptive
techniques, where the objective or effect is materially distorting behaviour by appreciably
impairing informed decision-making and causing or being reasonably likely to cause significant
harm.

It also prohibits exploiting vulnerabilities due to age, disability, or a specific social or
economic situation for that harmful behaviour-distorting purpose. Prohibited social-scoring uses
include evaluation or classification over time based on social behaviour or personal
characteristics where the resulting score causes unjustified, disproportionate, or
out-of-context detrimental treatment.

Other prohibited practices include criminal-risk assessment based solely on profiling or
personality traits; untargeted scraping of internet or CCTV facial images to create or expand
facial-recognition databases; emotion inference in workplaces and education except for medical or
safety reasons; and specified sensitive biometric categorisation. Real-time remote biometric
identification in publicly accessible spaces for law enforcement is prohibited subject to narrow,
strictly necessary statutory exceptions and safeguards.
