# Obligations of deployers of high-risk AI systems

Article 26 requires deployers to use high-risk systems according to instructions and assign human
oversight to people with the necessary competence, training, authority, and support. Where a
deployer controls input data, it must ensure that the data is relevant and sufficiently
representative for the intended purpose.

Deployers must monitor operation. If use may present a regulated risk, they must inform the
provider or distributor and market-surveillance authority and suspend use. Serious incidents must
also be reported. Deployers must retain automatically generated logs under their control for an
appropriate period of at least six months unless other Union or national law provides otherwise.

Before workplace use, an employer deploying a high-risk system must inform workers'
representatives and affected workers. Public-authority deployers have EU database registration
checks. Where applicable, deployers use provider information to perform a GDPR data protection
impact assessment. Additional safeguards apply to post-remote biometric identification in law
enforcement.
