# High-risk classification

Under Article 6(1), an AI system is high-risk when it is a product, or safety component of a
product, covered by Annex I Union harmonisation legislation and that product must undergo a
third-party conformity assessment before market placement or service. Article 6(2) additionally
classifies the use cases listed in Annex III as high-risk.

An Annex III system may fall outside high-risk classification under Article 6(3) if it does not
pose a significant risk of harm to health, safety, or fundamental rights and does not materially
influence decision-making. Examples include a narrow procedural task, improving a completed human
activity, detecting patterns without replacing or influencing a properly reviewed assessment, or
performing a preparatory task. Profiling of natural persons remains high-risk. A provider relying
on the exception must document the assessment and register the system.

Annex III includes permitted biometric identification, sensitive biometric categorisation, and
emotion recognition; safety components for critical infrastructure; specified education uses;
employment systems used to target job advertisements, analyse or filter applications, evaluate
candidates, make decisions affecting work relationships, allocate tasks, or monitor workers;
creditworthiness and life or health insurance risk/pricing; emergency dispatch and patient
triage; and specified law-enforcement, migration, asylum, justice, and democratic-process uses.
