# Provider duties for high-risk systems

Article 9 requires a documented, continuous, iterative risk-management system throughout the
high-risk system lifecycle. It must identify and analyse known and foreseeable risks, estimate
risks under intended use and reasonably foreseeable misuse, evaluate post-market information, and
adopt targeted controls. Residual risk must be acceptable, and systems must be tested against
predefined metrics and probabilistic thresholds before market placement or service.

Article 10 requires appropriate data-governance and management practices for training, validation,
and test datasets. These cover design choices, collection, preparation, assumptions, availability,
quantity, suitability, possible bias, and mitigation. Datasets must be relevant, sufficiently
representative, and, to the best extent possible, error-free and complete for their intended
purpose.

Article 13 requires operation transparent enough for deployers to interpret output and use it
appropriately. Instructions must be concise, complete, correct, clear, accessible, and
comprehensible, covering intended purpose, accuracy, robustness, cybersecurity, known risks,
capabilities, limitations, input specifications, output interpretation, and human oversight.

Article 14 requires effective human oversight proportionate to autonomy, risk, and use context.
Oversight personnel must understand limitations, detect anomalies, avoid automation bias,
interpret output, disregard or reverse output, and intervene or safely stop operation where
appropriate. Article 15 requires appropriate and consistent accuracy, robustness, and
cybersecurity throughout the lifecycle, with accuracy metrics declared in instructions.
