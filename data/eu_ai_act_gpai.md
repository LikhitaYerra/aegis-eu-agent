# General-purpose AI models

Article 51 classifies a general-purpose AI model as presenting systemic risk where it has high
impact capabilities evaluated with appropriate tools and methods or the Commission designates it
as having equivalent capability or impact. Training compute above 10^25 floating-point operations
creates a rebuttable presumption of high-impact capability.

Article 53 requires general-purpose model providers to maintain technical documentation covering
training, testing, and evaluation; give downstream system providers information needed to
understand capabilities and limitations and comply with the Act; establish a Union copyright-law
compliance policy; and publish a sufficiently detailed summary of training content using the AI
Office template. Limited documentation exceptions apply to qualifying free and open-source models,
but not models with systemic risk.

Article 55 adds obligations for providers of models with systemic risk: state-of-the-art model
evaluation and documented adversarial testing; assessment and mitigation of possible systemic
risks at Union level; tracking, documenting, and reporting serious incidents and corrective
measures without undue delay; and adequate cybersecurity for both the model and physical
infrastructure.
