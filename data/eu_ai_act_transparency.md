# Transparency obligations

Article 50(1) requires providers to design systems that interact directly with natural persons so
those people are informed that they are interacting with AI, unless this is obvious to a
reasonably well-informed, observant, and circumspect person in the circumstances and context.

Providers of systems that generate synthetic audio, image, video, or text content must generally
mark outputs in a machine-readable format so they are detectable as artificially generated or
manipulated. Technical solutions must be effective, interoperable, robust, and reliable as far as
technically feasible, accounting for content type, implementation cost, and the state of the art.

Deployers of emotion-recognition or biometric-categorisation systems must inform exposed people.
Deployers publishing deepfakes generally must disclose artificial generation or manipulation.
Deployers publishing generated or manipulated text to inform the public on matters of public
interest must generally disclose it, subject to exceptions including human review or editorial
control where a person holds editorial responsibility.

Required information must be clear and distinguishable, accessible, and provided no later than
the first interaction or exposure.
